Fall Gardening Preparation

1. Clean Up Your Garden

Start by removing dead or diseased plants from your garden. This helps prevent pests and diseases from overwintering and affecting next seasonâ€™s growth. Compost healthy plant material to create nutrient-rich soil for future use.

2. Prepare Your Soil

Enrich your soil by adding compost or well-rotted manure. This improves soil structure and adds vital nutrients that will break down over the winter, making your soil ready for spring planting.

3. Mulch Your Garden Beds

Apply a layer of mulch to your garden beds to help insulate the soil and protect plant roots from harsh winter conditions. Organic mulches such as shredded leaves or bark are excellent choices.

4. Plant Cover Crops

Sow cover crops such as clover or winter rye to improve soil health. These crops prevent erosion, suppress weeds, and add organic matter to the soil when tilled under in the spring.

5. Protect Perennial Plants

Wrap burlap around delicate perennials or add extra mulch around their bases to help them survive freezing temperatures. This helps ensure your plants come back strong in the spring.

6. Prepare Garden Tools

Clean, sharpen, and oil your gardening tools before storing them for the winter. Properly maintained tools will last longer and be ready for use when spring arrives.

By following these tips, you can ensure your garden is well-prepared for winter and ready to thrive when spring comes around.
